About CEMCA

In response to needs expressed by the Commonwealth countries of the Asian region for a more effective utilization of educational media resources for Distance Education, the Commonwealth of Learning (COL) established Commonwealth Educational Media Centre for Asia (CEMCA) in the year 1994. CEMCA, under a host country agreement signed between COL and Government of India (GoI) in 1998, has it’s headquarter in New Delhi. The GoI notified CEMCA as a diplomatic mission under section 3 of the United Nations (privileges and immunities) Act, 1947 (46 of 1947) vide The Gazette of India, Extraordinary, Part II, Section 3, Sub-section (ii) dated 10 February 2000.

CEMCA Working

The Commonwealth Educational Media Centre for Asia (CEMCA) serves as the regional unit of the Commonwealth of Learning. CEMCA promotes the meaningful, relevant and appropriate use of ICTs to serve the educational and training needs of Commonwealth member states of Asia. The strategic objective of CEMCA is to promote co-operation and collaboration in the use of electronic media resources for distance education.

The Advisory Council at CEMCA is responsible for a variety of things, including helping to shape the overall focus of the program, providing guidance on how to improve the program, and looking out for the best interests of the program.Representatives from Bangladesh, India, Maldives, Malaysia, Singapore and Sri Lanka currently serve on the Council.
